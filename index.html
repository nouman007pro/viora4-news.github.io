"Is US still helping Ukraine?"

"How Saudi Arabia helps Ukraine"

U.S. President Donald Trump announced that the United States has "just about" ended its suspension of intelligence sharing with Ukraine. This development comes as Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy travels to Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, for critical discussions with U.S. officials aimed at resolving the ongoing conflict with Russia. REUTERS The suspension of intelligence sharing and military aid was initially implemented to pressure Ukraine into engaging in peace talks with Russia. The upcoming talks in Riyadh will assess Ukraine's willingness to make concessions to end the war, with discussions also focusing on a potential minerals agreement between Washington and Kyiv. REUTERS President Trump expressed optimism about the forthcoming negotiations, stating, "We're going to make a lot of progress, I believe, this week." He also indicated that he believes Ukraine will sign the minerals agreement but emphasized the need for Ukraine to demonstrate a stronger commitment to peace. THE JERUSALEM POST In Riyadh, President Zelenskyy is scheduled to meet with Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman before the talks with U.S. officials. Zelenskyy has reiterated Ukraine's dedication to seeking peace and is preparing realistic proposals for negotiation. REUTERS The international community is closely monitoring these developments, as the outcomes of the Riyadh talks could significantly impact the trajectory of the conflict in Ukraine.
